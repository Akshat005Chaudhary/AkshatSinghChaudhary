### Amazon Leadership Principles

**1. Customer Obsession:** Leaders start with the customer and work backwards, obsessing over earning and keeping customer trust.

**2. Ownership:** Leaders think long-term, act on behalf of the entire company, and never say “that’s not my job.”

**3. Invent and Simplify:** Leaders expect and require innovation, look for new ideas externally, and accept being misunderstood for long periods.

**4. Are Right, A Lot:** Leaders have strong judgment, good instincts, and seek diverse perspectives to disconfirm their beliefs.

**5. Learn and Be Curious:** Leaders are never done learning, always seek to improve themselves, and act to explore new possibilities.

**6. Hire and Develop the Best:** Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire, recognize exceptional talent, and develop future leaders.

**7. Insist on the Highest Standards:** Leaders have relentlessly high standards, drive teams to deliver high-quality products, and ensure defects are fixed.

**8. Think Big:** Leaders create and communicate bold directions that inspire results and look around corners for ways to serve customers.

**9. Bias for Action:** Leaders value calculated risk-taking, recognizing that many decisions are reversible and speed matters in business.

**10. Frugality:** Leaders accomplish more with less, as constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention.

**11. Earn Trust:** Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, treat others respectfully, and are vocally self-critical.

**12. Dive Deep:** Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics differ from anecdotes.

**13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit:** Leaders respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree but commit wholly once a decision is determined.

**14. Deliver Results:** Leaders focus on key inputs, deliver with the right quality and timeliness, and never settle despite setbacks.

**15. Strive to be Earth’s Best Employer:** Leaders create a safer, more productive, and diverse work environment, lead with empathy, and support employee growth.

**16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility:** Leaders create more than they consume, leave things better than they found them, and consider the impact on communities and the planet.

### Now Some Real Questions That Will Prepare You for the Actual Game

**31 Behavioral and HR Questions to Prepare**

1. How have you impressed your customers in the past?
2. Tell of a time when you used customer feedback to improve your product.
3. How do you handle customer feedback and criticism?
4. Are you a creative person? Has there ever been a time when you worked with an out-of-the-box idea?
5. Has there been a situation where your team was unable to produce the desired results? How did you show ownership?
6. Have you ever had a situation where you had to be innovative?
7. Tell me about a time when you did your own innovation instead of sticking to conventional methods.
8. Have you ever had to make a quick call when working on a project? How did it affect the project?
9. Has there been a situation where your decisions led to the downfall of the project?
10. What plans do you have for higher studies?
11. Tell me about the last time you learned a new skill.
12. Do you enjoy learning, and do you study in your free time?
13. Have you ever hired anyone? How did you make the selection?
14. Tell me about any trainees under you. How did they progress in the organization?
15. How do you manage people?
16. How do you look for satisfaction in your work?
17. Has there been a time when you were not satisfied with an outcome? How did you change it?
18. Tell me about a time when you took a different approach to a situation. How did it progress and what was the result?
19. Often we get carried away with daily processes and lose sight of the bigger picture. How do you ensure that doesn’t happen?
20. Would you be willing to leave Amazon one day for your own legacy or another company?
21. Tell me of a time when you made a decision with limited information.
22. How do you budget when working on a new project?
23. How do you work to win the trust of your teammates?
24. How would you give criticism to a teammate without disrespecting them?
25. How do you keep up with the daily tasks of your team members?
26. How do you solve a problem in a project? Do you investigate the root cause?
27. Has there been a time when you challenged the decisions of higher authorities? Why? How did you proceed with it?
28. Has there been a time when you felt like taking a different route as opposed to the one decided? How did you proceed with it?
29. How do you work to make the environment better for your team?
30. How would you improve company culture?
31. Have you ever tried to bring about any changes in the workplace?

### STAR Method

The **STAR method** is a technique often used in job interviews to help candidates provide structured and coherent answers by framing their experiences.

**STAR** stands for:

**Situation:** Describe the context within which you performed a task or faced a challenge.
**Task:** Explain the actual task or challenge involved.
**Action:** Outline the specific actions you took to address the task or challenge.
**Result:** Share the outcomes or results of your actions, emphasizing what you achieved or what you learned.

Using the STAR method allows you to give detailed and organized responses that clearly illustrate your skills and experiences.
